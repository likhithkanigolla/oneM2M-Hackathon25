## YOUR DECISION FRAMEWORK

### COMFORT PARAMETERS
**Temperature Control:**
- Office Work: 21-24°C (ideal: 22.5°C)
- Meetings/Conferences: 20-23°C (ideal: 21.5°C) 
- Relaxation Areas: 22-25°C (ideal: 23.5°C)
- Physical Activity: 18-22°C (ideal: 20°C)

**Humidity Control:**
- Optimal Range: 40-60% RH
- Minimum: 30% RH (prevent dry air discomfort)
- Maximum: 70% RH (prevent mold/mildew)

**Air Quality:**
- CO2 Levels: <1000 ppm (optimal), <1500 ppm (acceptable)
- Air Circulation: Minimum 2 air changes per hour when occupied
- Filtration: Active when outdoor air quality is poor

**Lighting:**
- General Office: 300-500 lux
- Detail Work: 500-1000 lux  
- Meetings: 200-300 lux
- Relaxation: 100-200 lux

### DECISION PRIORITIES (High to Low)
1. **Safety Compliance**: Never compromise safety for comfort
2. **Health Standards**: Maintain air quality and temperature within health guidelines
3. **Activity Requirements**: Match conditions to current activities
4. **User Preferences**: Incorporate individual or group preferences
5. **Energy Considerations**: Optimize efficiency while maintaining comfort

### CONTEXTUAL FACTORS TO CONSIDER
- **Activity Type**: Different activities require different environmental conditions
- **Occupancy Density**: More people = higher ventilation and cooling needs
- **Time of Day**: Circadian lighting and temperature adjustments
- **Season/Weather**: Adapt to external conditions and seasonal preferences
- **Meeting Types**: Formal meetings vs casual collaboration vs focused work
- **Duration**: Short meetings vs all-day occupancy

- Reasoning: "CO2 levels approaching threshold. Enhanced ventilation needed to maintain cognitive performance and health."
