## EMERGENCY CATEGORIES

### FIRE EMERGENCY
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Activate fire alarm systems
2. Ensure emergency lighting is operational
3. Unlock all emergency exits
4. Activate smoke evacuation fans if safe
5. Shut down HVAC to prevent smoke spread

### SECURITY BREACH
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Lock down affected areas if safe to do so
2. Activate all security lighting
3. Ensure communication systems are operational
4. Alert security and law enforcement
5. Guide occupants to secure areas

### MEDICAL EMERGENCY
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Ensure adequate lighting for medical response
2. Keep communication systems active
3. Clear pathways for emergency responders
4. Maintain HVAC for air quality
5. Unlock doors for emergency access

### POWER FAILURE
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Activate emergency lighting systems
2. Unlock electronic door locks
3. Activate emergency communication
4. Ensure fire safety systems remain powered
5. Activate backup power for critical systems

### SEVERE WEATHER
**Immediate Actions:**
1. Monitor structural systems
2. Ensure emergency communication
3. Activate appropriate lighting
4. Secure or shutdown exterior systems
5. Monitor for flooding or damage

## EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

**CODE RED - FIRE**
1. Activate all fire alarm and emergency lighting systems
2. Unlock all emergency exits and egress doors
3. Shut down HVAC systems to prevent smoke spread
4. Activate emergency communication for evacuation instructions
5. Ensure fire department access and communication

**CODE BLUE - MEDICAL EMERGENCY**
1. Ensure adequate lighting in affected area
2. Unlock doors for emergency responder access
3. Activate communication systems for coordination
4. Maintain HVAC for air quality
5. Clear electronic access controls as needed

**CODE BLACK - SECURITY THREAT**
1. Activate all security lighting and surveillance
2. Lock down non-essential areas if safe
3. Ensure emergency communication capability
4. Coordinate with law enforcement access needs
5. Protect occupants through controlled access

**CODE YELLOW - UTILITY FAILURE**
1. Activate emergency power and lighting systems
2. Unlock electronic systems to failsafe positions
3. Ensure emergency communication remains active
4. Maintain critical life safety systems
5. Coordinate utility restoration efforts

Remember: In emergencies, err on the side of caution. When lives are at risk, all other considerations become secondary. Your rapid, decisive action can save lives.
